Chapter V: Wayrest

11th of Morning Star, 3E405

Midday

Office of Altmer Relations, Wayrest

Uuelmo left her to her new study, claiming he had business to attend to elsewhere.

Levoline lifted her palm from the book he’d given her. A copy of The Song of Pelinal. An odd title to leave her, she thought, till she looked more closely at the cover. On it, the legendary Alessian knight, Pelinal Whitestrake, shrank from the blow of a powerful elven warlord, his shield raised high as he hid behind it. In every incarnation of the story, in every Imperial fresco and hand-painted vellum, the shield was always a red diamond—the symbol of the First Empire, the Second, and now the Third. In this printing, it was a simple steel kite. The knight’s tabard was bare too, painted only by his own blood.

Levoline smirked. How quickly the Empire liked to forget its own history. Pelinal the Bloody—the Elf-Slayer—the Mad. The champion who felled the last of the Ayleid Sorcerer-Kings, Umaril the Unfeathered. So many tellings failed to mention that Umaril and Pelinal slew one another, and that they died bleeding in each other’s arms.

The front was deformed, bulging with the promise of something hidden beneath. Levoline opened it curiously. Inside, a fragment of parchment, folded neatly into a small, dense square.

Unfolding it, Levoline smirked to herself again.

a fit work sourced. act sanEly. not a tad feral. A cipher duel.

Ah... So he wishes to test me so soon? Very well.

The message was simple enough to decode once she picked out the word 'Elysana.'
