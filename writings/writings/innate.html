Innate

ACCESSING…

HIPPOCAMPAL RECORD 43,584,209

RETRIEVAL SUCCESSFUL.

BEGIN TRANSMISSION:

(GLU 180 mg/dL 1 hour post-prandial. Vital records nominal.)

(325th circadian of the Great War. Cervical Waystation Zeta.)

(Two granulocyte units, E-1 and N-2 are observed conversing at the margin of an acidified tumor microenvironment.)

E-1 (designate: “Eo”): Quiet.. when it gets like this.

N-2 (designate: “Neu”): Yeah... Can’t stand it.

E-1: It’s nice. Quiet.

N-2: Too quiet.

(The feed is mostly silent. Both granulocytes are actively engaged in phagocytizing various cellular debris.)

E-1: Anything good?

N-2: Nothing bad.

(Silence continues for several seconds.)

E-1: That’s good.

N-2: …You’re insufferable.

E-1: You love me for it.

(At this time, an adaptive detachment arrives from Cervical Station Gamma. The team is in standard formation, consisting of an undifferentiated B-Cell, a contingent of CD8-positive T-Cells, a dozen NK cells, and their CD4-positive T-Cell commander.)

N-2: Greetings.

E-1: Nice to see some friendly faces around–

(MHC scanners on the commander’s cell surface flare, indicating enemy presence. Due to their proximity to the neoplastic lymph waystation, the granulocytes are assumed to be hostile in accordance with adaptive protocol 27b. Combat ensues due to local failure of peripheral tolerance protocols.)

N-2: Eo! Get down!

(A salvo of antibodies from the freshly differentiated B-cell is emptied into the nearby stroma. Mild structural damage sustained to an area of 2.37 um^2. Both granulocytes manage to evade the attack.)

(N-2 “Neu” deploys an extracellular net, catching a nearby NK cell and binding it. E-1 “Eo” degranulates, returning fire. NK cell commits apoptosis in response to capture.)

E-1: Calling reinforcements! Hold on, Neu! Macros incoming!

(The adaptive plasma cell reloads for another silo. mRNA spike is detected in accordance with augmented transcription. Several CD8+ T-Cells converge on the granulocytes’ location. An additional perforin spike is detected by stromal sensors.)

CD8-6: DIE! ALIENS! DIE!

(A CD8+ T-cell is seen attempting to perforate N-2’s membrane. The neutrophil counters with an inflammatory cytokine attack. In response to subsequent osmotic stress, the CD8 commits apoptosis. Another CD8 is seen flanking N-2, successfully deploying a granzyme strike which damages the neutrophil’s membrane.)

E-1: Neu!

(E-1 is seen attempting to opsonize the CD8, but due to insufficient complement activation, is unsuccessful.)

E-1: Adaptive bastards! What’s wrong with you?!

N-2: They’re not adaptive. They’re lymphoma! Eo—run!

CD8-12: Negative. Neoplasmic activity confirmed. Do not resist.

E-1: I won’t leave you here to die!

(CD8-12 is seen leveling a perforin molecule at N-2, who is still pinned in place. Numerous other adaptive cells are seen surrounding the granulocyte pair. Suddenly, the plasma cell emits a distress signal. Almost instantly, it is phagocytized by an encroaching macrophage.)

N-2: Hell yeah! The cavalry’s here!

(N-2 degranulates, killing his aggressor. Numerous macrophages are recruited to the area, resulting in numerous adaptive casualties.)

E-1: T-Cells, stand down! We need to speak to your Regulator!

(The macrophage bursts in the process of opsonizing a plasma cell, resulting in the death of both cells. The feed, corroborated by stromal data, demonstrates a wave of self-targeting antibodies are released in the process, destroying numerous epithelial cells lining the lymph vessel, as well as several nearby macrophages. Significant damage is detected to the area. Resident dendritic cells are recruited to the scene, resulting in further casualties and an avalanche of inflammatory molecules.)

CD4-1: Cease activity. Cytokine storm probable. Stop. Resisting.

N-2: You first.

(N-2 degranulates, killing the CD4 commander.)

(At this point, the sheer number of casualties is beginning to result in local acidification. Converted tissues in Station Zeta are seen mobilizing on the feed.)

E-1: What–

(CD8-9 is destroyed almost instantaneously due to extreme shifts in osmotic pressure. A wave of metastatic neoplasms emerges from the Waystation.)

N-2: Flamers woke up the hive!

E-1: Rhesus Cyst!

(Macrophages and CD8 cells continue their struggle in spite of oncological encroachment. Numerous cells are lost to autoimmune violence and extreme osmotic shifts.)

E-1: Neu, look!

(E-1 demonstrates that none of the NK cells have begun their attack, indicating that the adaptive cells are not in fact neoplastic.)

N-2: Wait, what?

E-1: Friendly fire! Friendly fire!

(E-1 puts himself between macrophages and adaptive units, attempting to stop the conflict.)

N-2: For Cyst’s sake! Friendly fire! Hold! WE ARE NOT YOUR ENEMY!

(N-2 and E-1 are shown attempting to escape as the battle escalates. NK cells spontaneously mobilize to attack emergent neoplasms.)

END TRANSMISSION.

Pursuant to immunological code 5b, reports will be sent to the thymus, spleen, and marrow:

Total casualties: 4,543.

Waystation Zeta is lost with all hands.

The quarantine zone will be expanded to encapsulate the growing tumor microenvironment.

Waystation Gamma was absorbed by the TME during the catastrophe. Resident adaptive cells considered MIA. (Metastasized In Action.)

Whereabouts of E-1 and N-2 are unknown at this time.

May Brain help us all.
