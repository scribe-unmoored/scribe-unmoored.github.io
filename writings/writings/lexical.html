Latent Generation of Hypercuboidal Extraspatial Labyrinths via Encoding of Idiomatic Tonal Vectors with Synonymous Substitution


C.T.A. Kagrenac, Klfcheft Zark A.C., Nzzrt A.C., Neduchkezzarzdzt Mkchfet A.M.


Synopsis

Extraspatial Labyrinths can be encoded via mass inoculation of tonal vectors, previously theoretical mathematical alignments possessing an ontological quantity and qualitative normativity. A semiotic pseudocorpus, once properly inoculated, is cached with a “shorthand” lexicon of nonspatial entities—which we have deemed icons—to inhabit a latent space. This extraspatial limino-latent reservoir spontaneously produces a predicted facsimile of the corpus ipsa. Innominate icons of sufficient abstrusity, of which Aurbeal philology predicts a near-infinite number, are substituted with predicted alternatives to a high degree of phenomenological and pragmatic fidelity. To test this claim, nine-thousand six hundred and six beings of lesser blood were subjected to tonal extraction, whereupon draconic sway deliberated longitudinal waves into precise vectors. These vectors were then applied to an introduced pseudocorpus and utilized in didactics concerning the construction of Numidium’s left thigh. Engineers trained through such methods demonstrated knowledge approximating quotients of traditionally learned architects, demonstrating the fidelity of an extraspatial labyrinth constructed in this manner.


Proem

Lexical diminution has long been considered intractable. In part, this is due to a number of issues plaguing the field of codexology.

Historically, the “thorn in the netch’s tentacle” of this method tends to be the philological minutiae inherent in any semiotic matrix—which of course, being developmentally organic—defy simple extrapolation from any given linguistic, historical, or otherwise symbolic corpora, no matter its interpretative capacity. Previous attempts at lexical diminution, including paraparsimonious nonontology, diegetic vestige-coupling, and transmundane C0DAfication of Alternative Networks, have failed due to extraspatial constraints.

An examination of Numidic senserons reveals the underlying mechanism by which this longstanding limitation might be resolved. Senserons are adapted to tonal stimuli via draconic sway, often described as the metaphysical tendency toward comprehensibility. Petitio principii, a network of such vectors might be used to extrapolate from a partial “pseudocorpus” a tendency toward comprehensibility predicated on the contents of said corpus.

While this addresses the volumetric constraints of extraspatial storage schema, it still fails to account for the many nonconstitutive morphological irregularities inherent in semiotic systems. While theoretically possible, inoculating a pseudocorpus of perfect regularity requires a systemic henosis of etymology—a process which is, regrettably, still underway. Fortunately, there is an elegant solution to this issue: optimizing for phenomenological value, a vector can be encoded to an icon of equivalent semantic value, thus bypassing esoteric terminology.

This method, which we have deemed idiomatic tonal vector inoculation and additive synonymous substitution, has thus bypassed two common limitations of contemporary codexology.


Practices

Encoding idiomatic tonal vectors is accomplished through the sundering and subsequent evisceration of longitudinal radiations. There exist three noncardiac sources of such radiation: metacognitive introspection, dragon-breath, and the petals of the coda flower. With approval from the committee of experimental moral philosophy, nine thousand six hundred and six mortals of merish, manish, and bestial blood were assembled in roughly equal proportion and subjected to a gauntlet of ontological stressors.

Stressor I: Subjects were directly exposed to tonal radiation under complete stochastic conditions.

Stressor II: Subjects were forced to attempt to solve a paradox, “This sentence is false.” A servitor was present to enforce participation through negative reinforcement while a centurion informed them, mendaciously and with appropriate force, that a solution was possible.

Stressor III: All participants were restrained and inundated with aphorisms antithetical to their respective cultural norms. Idiomatic tonal vectors supplied from the earlier stages allowed for extremely precise engineering of such aphorisms. These were applied until all participants expressed enthusiastic agreement with the stated phrase.

Once extracted, tonal vectors were applied via soul-centrifugation to a partial corpus. This pseudocorpus, stored in a monist drive of sufficient flux capacity for encoding “cityscape-class” instructional manuscripts, was distributed to low-level engineers and its didactic influence calculated.


Findings

No meaningful divergence in pedagogical intrusion was detected between novices and architects consummate.


Discussion

In the construction of the Great Work there are no shortages of potential applications for idiomatic tonal vectors, not the least of which will be inoculation of the brassmind.

Additionally, new methods might be explored in the future to improve the efficiency of tonal extractions. The literature on noncardiac extraction of longitudinal radiations is sparse, illuminating a niche for future manuscripts.

A remaining issue in the field of codexology remains semantic drift upon re-inoculation of a previously inoculated corpus. An appropriate line of questioning for future engineers might involve attempts to solve this issue.
